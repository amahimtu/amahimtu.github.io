DrumMachine Lab

Explore the world's most iconic drum machines.


Overview.

DrumMachine Lab is envisioned as a smartphone, tablet, and desktop application that allows users to explore and play software versions of the world's most iconic drum machines. this project will walk you through my ui/ux design process leading up to the clickable prototype and video prototype of the app.
Tools used: Adobe: XD, Illustrator, Photoshop; HTML, CSS.

The Process.

My interest in music production led me to come up with the concept for DrumMachine Lab. The idea behind the app is that users, such as music producers and fans of classic drum machines, can try out software versions of typically analog/hardware drum machines on their smartphones, tablets, or desktops. For this project, I will be showcasing the smartphone version of the app.
Why might someone use DrumMachine Lab? They can:

  • Try out and play iconic drum machines.
  • Design sounds and produce beats and rhythms via drum machines.
  • Connect the app to their preferred Digital Audio Workstation (DAW), and save and export MIDI and audio files.


Goals.

Some broad goals I had in mind for the user as I started this project:

  • Exploration
  • Sound design
  • Entertainment
  • Trying out software drum machines before investing in the 'real' thing
  • Save space
  • Save money
  • Become a better beatmaker/producer


User Personas.

I came up with some user personas, i.e, potential DrumMachine Lab users. This got me thinking, “Who is the target audience for this app?”


Broadly, the app’s target audience is people between the ages of 18-50 with an interest in music production, beatmaking, and/or drum machines. Of course, if I were to develop and publish the app, more market research would be needed to narrow down a target audience.


Notes from Research and Testing.

Since this is a personal project and the application is aimed at a fairly niche group of users, I was not able to conduct extensive user testing. However, speaking to five musicians who are casual music producers—all recent graduates of universities in Ohio and Michigan—it appears they are inclined to use the app as long as it is affordable, fun, and easy to use. Producers often spend a lot of money on music software and plugins, so the app would need to be clever in its monetization strategy if it were to come to fruition.

The app would ideally use officially licensed software versions of drum machines from companies like Roland, Arturia, and Korg. If users enjoy the software machines, they may look to buy the ‘real’ hardware machines from these companies, so hardware manufacturers are incentivized to pay DrumMachine Lab to feature their drum machines.

Based on my research, while there are some online drum machines, there does not appear to be a singular platform for producers to be able to browse and play through a comprehensive collection of existing drum machines.

Reflection: Advantages & Drawbacks

After creating the prototype, I reflected on DrumMachine Lab and thought of some advantages and drawbacks for users. There are more pros than cons, which is always a good sign.


Drawbacks:

  • Software drum machines may not have the full functionality/complexity of hardware ones, so the app would have limited features.
  • Smartphones have a relatively small screen so it’s hard to play the machines. This is addressed by iPad and desktop versions of the app.
  • Licensing with well-known companies would be difficult. But it can encourage more users to get the app if products are officially licensed.

Advantages:
  • Users can try out software versions of drum machines before before buying physical ones (They are expensive).
  • Users can play on the go.
  • Users can record and export MIDI/Audio.
  • Tutorials and ease of use.
  • The app can help bring vintage drum machines back into the mainstream, like the rise of record players and vinyl in recent years.
  • DrumMachine Lab is great for music enthusiasts, experienced producers, and drum machine fans, but at the same time, the app could encourage novice producers to learn more about drum machines, beatmaking, and music production.
