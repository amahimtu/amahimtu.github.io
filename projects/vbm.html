Vintage Band Merch

Memorabilia for the realest fans.


Overview.

Vintage Band Merch (VBM) is envisioned as the go-to e-commerce store for the resale of retro and vintage memorabilia. Musicians and music enthusiasts the world over love band merch (I know I do), but merch is often seasonal, and—especially for more popular artists—can get sold out quickly and are high in value.

For instance, you can find Radiohead shirts from some of their old tours being sold for hundreds of dollars (roughly 4-5x their original value) online through websites like eBay and Etsy, meaning there is a market for cool vintage merch but no dedicated platform like VBM.

VBM appeals to your nostalgic longings by providing you with original vintage merchandise from huge bands like Matchbox Twenty to relatively low-key artists like Killing Time.

Note: This was a fun and challenging personal project I undertook to prototype the website for Vintage Band Merch, whereas the actual site does not exist yet. However, I do own the domain (vintagebandmerch.com). I will walk you through my ui/ux design process leading up to the prototype of the website.

Tools used: Figma, Adobe Photoshop, Lucidchart, HTML, CSS, Bootstrap.


Sitemap.

I started this project by developing the sitemap for the website, taking inspiration from the information architecture of current existing music merchandise online stores. The sitemap is color-coded according to groups of activities that a user would interact with and experience.


Wireframe(s).

I then drew up some wireframes using just a notebook and pencil as shown below. The homepage consists of four sections: clickable hero image slider, a featured products section with carousel feature, 'about us,' and a site footer, which features a contact form, sitemap information, social media links, contact information, and copyright text.


UI Layout.

After drawing my wireframes, I utilized Figma to create a UI layout to shape the website. I decided to go for a 12-column layout so that the site would be compatible with frameworks like Bootstrap. Additionally, the spaciousness provided by this kind of layout would lend the site a clean, contemporary feel. Even though most of the merch is vintage, I wanted to balance out the retro feel (via artifacts like the logo) with a modern design.


Prototype.

Using the UI layout as a blueprint, I designed the mockup. I created the VBM logo—consisting of a vinyl record with the letters VBM—with Adobe Photoshop. For the hero images, I used artwork of Boards of Canada, Jimi Hendrix, and Steven Wilson, respectively.

After creating all the sections of the site, I prototyped the dropdown menus, hero image slider, and product carousel. Check out the prototype pathways outlining various combinations of user interactions.

Reflection & Notes.

Since the design of the site is fairly intricate (for ex., it contains hero images, carousels, interactive contact forms, etc.), I spent a considerable amount of time prototyping and utilizing Figma’s overlay feature, which might explain the somewhat complex network of overlays and user interactions.

If you tried out the prototype or watched the video demonstration, however, you should find the user experience simple and intuitive.

As a musician, this was such an enjoyable passion project to work on, as well as a great learning experience. I hope to make Vintage Band Merch a fully realized e-commerce business one day.

Thanks for checking out this project. I welcome feedback!
